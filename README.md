# Stochastic Volatility in Options Trading

The Black-Scholes model is famous for being one of the standard option pricing models, and something I learned to derive with differential equations.However, one distinct feature is that the Black-Scholes assumes a constant volatility. Like all random things in the market, that is not true and volatility follows a random walk process as well. Stochastic Volatility comes into play here, and an extension of the Black Scholes is the Heston Model for options pricing, a prcier that does take into account stochastic vol.

In today's time where volatility is spiking all other different asset classes, oil and commodities would be a good example to use. This project will:

1. Create a volatility surface
2. Generate the market's future price distribution based on option prices
3. Compare results from Black-Scholes vs. Heston
